<p><b>The National</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Indie_rock" title="Indie rock">indie rock</a> band formed in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, United States, in 1999, and currently based in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>. The band's lyrics, which have been described as "dark, melancholy and difficult to interpret",<sup id="cite_ref-thescenestar_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thescenestar-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> are written and sung by <a href="/wiki/Matt_Berninger" title="Matt Berninger">Matt Berninger</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Baritone" title="Baritone">baritone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The band's music is composed and performed by <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Dessner" title="Aaron Dessner">Aaron Dessner</a> (guitar and keyboard), <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Dessner" title="Bryce Dessner">Bryce Dessner</a> (guitar), Scott Devendorf (bass) and Bryan Devendorf (drums). The band has recorded six studio albums; the most recent, <i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_Will_Find_Me" title="Trouble Will Find Me">Trouble Will Find Me</a></i>, was released in May 2013 and was nominated in the 2014 Grammys for Best Alternative Album.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history_.281991.E2.80.932000.29">Early history (1991–2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history (1991–2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1991, Matt Berninger and Scott Devendorf met while attending the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cincinnati" title="University of Cincinnati">University of Cincinnati</a>'s DAAP college of graphic design program,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> where they also met Mike Brewer, <a href="/wiki/Casey_Reas" title="Casey Reas" class="mw-redirect">Casey Reas</a> and Jeff Salem.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Together, the five of them formed the lo-fi garage band <i>Nancy</i>, named after Berninger's mother, aspiring to sound like <a href="/wiki/Pavement_(band)" title="Pavement (band)">Pavement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The band was together for five years, but only released one album, <i>Ruther 3429</i>, on Wife Records before breaking up after Berninger, Devendorf, Reas and Salem moved to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Bryan, Bryce and Aaron were childhood friends who played in several bands together over the years. When their last effort, <i>Project Nim</i>, broke up in 1998, they joined Matt and Scott in Brooklyn via the Devendorf relationship.</p>
<p>When the band was formed in 1999, it was called <i>The National</i>, although the domain name of the band's website is americanmary.com because, according to Matt Berninger in an interview with <i>Sixeyes</i>, "[i]t's a song off our first record. We never thought of changing the (website) name, although we should have."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Several of the members continued to work day jobs while performing free Sunday night shows regularly at the highly regarded NYC Lower East Side venue, <a href="/wiki/Luna_Lounge" title="Luna Lounge">Luna Lounge</a>, throughout the early years, including being involved in New York's <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_bubble" title="Dot-com bubble">dot-com boom</a> in the late 1990s.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_National_.282001.E2.80.932002.29"><i>The National</i> (2001–2002)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The National (2001–2002)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Their first album <i><a href="/wiki/The_National_(album)" title="The National (album)">The National</a></i> was eventually released in 2001 on <a href="/wiki/Brassland_Records" title="Brassland Records">Brassland Records</a>, a label founded by band members <a href="/wiki/Aaron_and_Bryce_Dessner" title="Aaron and Bryce Dessner">Aaron and Bryce Dessner</a>, along with their friend <a href="/wiki/Alec_Hanley_Bemis" title="Alec Hanley Bemis">Alec Hanley Bemis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> When reviewing the album, Jason MacNeil of <i><a href="/wiki/No_Depression_(magazine)" title="No Depression (magazine)">No Depression</a></i> wrote, "...The National has created nearly a dozen picture-perfect <a href="/wiki/Americana_music" title="Americana music" class="mw-redirect">Americana</a> bar-soaked gems with its debut album. From the opening notes of 'Beautiful Head', the delicate line between polished <a href="/wiki/Roots_rock" title="Roots rock">roots-oriented pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alt-country" title="Alt-country" class="mw-redirect">alt-country</a> has rarely been walked so deliberately with the payoff so favorable." <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sad_Songs_for_Dirty_Lovers_and_Cherry_Tree_.282003.E2.80.932004.29"><i>Sad Songs for Dirty Lovers</i> and <i>Cherry Tree</i> (2003–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sad Songs for Dirty Lovers and Cherry Tree (2003–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The National's second album <i><a href="/wiki/Sad_Songs_for_Dirty_Lovers" title="Sad Songs for Dirty Lovers">Sad Songs for Dirty Lovers</a></i>, released in 2003, was the band's first collaboration with record producers, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Heck" title="Paul Heck">Paul Heck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Katis" title="Peter Katis">Peter Katis</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-brassssfdl_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brassssfdl-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> who would later also produce the band's critically acclaimed albums, <i><a href="/wiki/Alligator_(The_National_album)" title="Alligator (The National album)">Alligator</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Boxer_(The_National_album)" title="Boxer (The National album)" class="mw-redirect">Boxer</a></i>. After the release of the album, renowned DJ <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Lenoir" title="Bernard Lenoir">Bernard Lenoir</a> invited them to perform on his Black Sessions twice on <a href="/wiki/France_Inter" title="France Inter">France Inter</a>. Publications such as <i><a href="/wiki/Uncut_(magazine)" title="Uncut (magazine)">Uncut</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i> named it an album of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-brassssfdl_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brassssfdl-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, they released the <i><a href="/wiki/Cherry_Tree_(EP)" title="Cherry Tree (EP)">Cherry Tree</a></i> EP which included live favorite "About Today," as well as "All the Wine," which would appear on their next record. The release of the EP garnered further success and landed them on a successful tour with <a href="/wiki/The_Walkmen" title="The Walkmen">The Walkmen</a>.</p>
<p>Also in 2004, the band quit their day jobs and signed to a new label, <a href="/wiki/Beggars_Banquet_Records" title="Beggars Banquet Records">Beggars Banquet Records</a>, because the process of running their own label was becoming "too complicated."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alligator_.282005.E2.80.932006.29"><i>Alligator</i> (2005–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Alligator (2005–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Their first album on Beggars Banquet, <i><a href="/wiki/Alligator_(The_National_album)" title="Alligator (The National album)">Alligator</a></i>, was released in 2005. The album was met with much critical acclaim and featured highly in "Album of the Year" charts in the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Insound" title="Insound">Insound</a>, <i>Uncut</i>, and many other publications.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> The album allowed the band increased exposure. <i><a href="/wiki/NME" title="NME">NME</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a> ranked <i>Alligator</i> as a top album of the 2000s.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><i>Alligator</i> brought the band increased attendance at concerts, including sold-out shows at <a href="/wiki/The_Troubadour_(Los_Angeles)" title="The Troubadour (Los Angeles)" class="mw-redirect">The Troubadour</a> in Los Angeles and <a href="/wiki/Webster_Hall" title="Webster Hall">Webster Hall</a> in New York. They also played at numerous festivals including the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Music_Festival" title="Pitchfork Music Festival">Pitchfork Music Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reading_and_Leeds_Festival" title="Reading and Leeds Festival" class="mw-redirect">Reading and Leeds Festivals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pukkelpop" title="Pukkelpop">Pukkelpop</a>, and more.</p>
<p><i>Alligator</i> went on to sell over 200,000 copies worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-en-us.nielsen.com_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-en-us.nielsen.com-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Boxer.2C_A_Skin.2C_a_Night_and_The_Virginia_EP_.282007.E2.80.932009.29"><i>Boxer</i>, <i>A Skin, a Night</i> and <i>The Virginia EP</i> (2007–2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Boxer, A Skin, a Night and The Virginia EP (2007–2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Their fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Boxer_(The_National_album)" title="Boxer (The National album)" class="mw-redirect">Boxer</a></i>, was released on May 22, 2007, and also received widespread critical praise.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The album features contributions from various guest artists, including <a href="/wiki/Sufjan_Stevens" title="Sufjan Stevens">Sufjan Stevens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doveman" title="Doveman">Doveman</a> (a.k.a. Thomas Bartlett). It was voted as the No.&#160;2 best album of the year by <a href="/wiki/Stereogum" title="Stereogum">Stereogum.com</a> and the No.&#160;1 album of the year by <i><a href="/wiki/Paste_(magazine)" title="Paste (magazine)">Paste Magazine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> The song "Slow Show" from <i>Boxer</i> was featured on the <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i>, as well as on <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/One_Tree_Hill_(TV_series)" title="One Tree Hill (TV series)">One Tree Hill</a></i> in its fifth season. The song "Start a War" was featured on the international science fiction series <i><a href="/wiki/Defying_Gravity_(TV_series)" title="Defying Gravity (TV series)">Defying Gravity</a></i>, ABC's <i><a href="/wiki/Brothers_%26_Sisters_(2006_TV_series)" title="Brothers &amp; Sisters (2006 TV series)">Brothers and Sisters</a></i>, FOX's <i><a href="/wiki/House_(TV_series)" title="House (TV series)">House</a></i>, NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Parenthood_(2010_TV_series)" title="Parenthood (2010 TV series)">Parenthood</a></i>, NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Friday_Night_Lights_(TV_series)" title="Friday Night Lights (TV series)">Friday Night Lights (TV series)</a></i>and Lionsgates film "Warrior". The track "Fake Empire" was featured in the Season 2 Premiere of the HBO Series <a href="/wiki/Hung_(TV_series)" title="Hung (TV series)">Hung</a> and on the 3rd episode of season 2 of NBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Chuck_(TV_series)" title="Chuck (TV series)">Chuck</a></i> and also in the pilot episode of <i><a href="/wiki/Southland_(TV_series)" title="Southland (TV series)">Southland</a></i>. An instrumental version of the song<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> was featured in <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s campaign video "Signs of Hope and Change" during his <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama_presidential_campaign,_2008" title="Barack Obama presidential campaign, 2008">2008 United States presidential campaign</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> and the song was also played at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Democratic_National_Convention" title="2008 Democratic National Convention">2008 Democratic National Convention</a>.</p>
<p>On September 26, 2007, the band performed "Apartment Story" on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Late_Late_Show_with_Craig_Ferguson" title="The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson">The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson</a></i>.</p>
<p>In the summer of 2008, along with <a href="/wiki/Modest_Mouse" title="Modest Mouse">Modest Mouse</a>, they opened for <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a> on the promotional tour for the R.E.M. album <i><a href="/wiki/Accelerate_(R.E.M._album)" title="Accelerate (R.E.M. album)">Accelerate</a></i>. That summer they also played many festivals in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Coachella_Valley_Music_and_Arts_Festival" title="Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival">Coachella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roskilde_Festival" title="Roskilde Festival">Roskilde</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sasquatch_Festival" title="Sasquatch Festival" class="mw-redirect">Sasquatch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glastonbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haldern_Pop" title="Haldern Pop">Haldern Pop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_Werchter" title="Rock Werchter">Rock Werchter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optimus_Alive!" title="Optimus Alive!">Optimus Alive!</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxegen_Festival" title="Oxegen Festival" class="mw-redirect">Oxegen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Festival_Internacional_de_Benic%C3%A0ssim" title="Festival Internacional de Benicàssim">Benicàssim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lowlands_(music_festival)" title="Lowlands (music festival)" class="mw-redirect">Lowlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/O2_Wireless_Festival" title="O2 Wireless Festival" class="mw-redirect">O2 Wireless</a>, <a href="/wiki/T_in_the_Park" title="T in the Park">T in the Park</a>, All Points West, and <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a>.</p>
<p><i>Boxer</i> made numerous "album of the decade" lists, including <a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_Media" title="Pitchfork Media">Pitchfork Media</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Aquarium Drunkard</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Paste</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> and more.</p>
<p>In May 2008, the band released their first feature-length documentary film entitled <i>A Skin, a Night</i> on DVD. The film, directed by filmmaker <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Moon" title="Vincent Moon">Vincent Moon</a>, documents the lives of the band surrounding the recording of <i>Boxer</i> and just before a show at the London venue <a href="/wiki/Koko_(venue)" title="Koko (venue)" class="mw-redirect">Koko</a>. Vincent Moon's film was widely disregarded by fans who had hoped the film would feature the band and their music more directly, rather than artful attempts. Along with the release of the DVD was a CD collection of B-sides and rarities titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Virginia_EP" title="The Virginia EP">The Virginia EP</a></i>.</p>
<p>The National's collaboration with Vincent Moon began long before the filming of <i>A Skin, a Night</i>. Vincent Moon discovered the band after the release of their first album and became friends with its members after a show at Paris's La Guinguette Pirate. Soon after this meeting, Moon filmed his first music videos ever, which were for The National's songs "Daughters of the Soho Riots" and "Lit Up".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Moon's photography also appears on the cover for <i>Alligator</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="High_Violet_.282010.E2.80.932012.29"><i>High Violet</i> (2010–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: High Violet (2010–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 17, 2009, a compilation album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Was_the_Night" title="Dark Was the Night">Dark Was the Night</a></i> was produced by Aaron and Bryce Dessner and released by <a href="/wiki/4AD" title="4AD">4AD</a> (the band's new label after Beggars Banquet Records merged into 4AD). The two-disc, 31-track compilation was released for the benefit of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>, and featured a new song by The National and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Muhly" title="Nico Muhly">Nico Muhly</a> titled "So Far Around the Bend." In the same year, The National collaborated with <a href="/wiki/St._Vincent_(musician)" title="St. Vincent (musician)">St. Vincent</a> to contribute a cover of <a href="/wiki/Crooked_Fingers" title="Crooked Fingers">Crooked Fingers</a>' "Sleep All Summer" to the <a href="/wiki/Merge_Records" title="Merge Records">Merge Records</a> compilation <i>Score! 20 Years of Merge Records: The Covers!</i>. On May 6, 2009, The National performed "So Far Around the Bend" on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Jimmy_Fallon" title="Late Night with Jimmy Fallon">Late Night with Jimmy Fallon</a></i>.</p>
<p>The National contributed a track to <i><a href="/wiki/Ciao_My_Shining_Star:_The_Songs_of_Mark_Mulcahy" title="Ciao My Shining Star: The Songs of Mark Mulcahy">Ciao My Shining Star: The Songs of Mark Mulcahy</a></i> in September 2009, an album in support of the former <a href="/wiki/Polaris_(band)" title="Polaris (band)">Polaris</a> frontman, who lost his wife. They covered the Polaris track "Ashamed of the Story I Told", from their album <i><a href="/wiki/Music_from_The_Adventures_of_Pete_%26_Pete" title="Music from The Adventures of Pete &amp; Pete">Music from The Adventures of Pete &amp; Pete</a></i>.</p>
<p>On March 10, 2010, the band performed "Terrible Love", the lead track from <i>High Violet</i>, on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_with_Jimmy_Fallon" title="Late Night with Jimmy Fallon">Late Night with Jimmy Fallon</a></i>. On March 24, the band released <i><a href="/wiki/Bloodbuzz_Ohio" title="Bloodbuzz Ohio">Bloodbuzz Ohio</a></i>, the first single from the album, for free download at the official <i>High Violet</i> website.<sup id="cite_ref-highviolet.com_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highviolet.com-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/High_Violet" title="High Violet">High Violet</a></i> was released on May 11, 2010 in the US to widespread critical acclaim.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> The album also debuted with first week sales topping charts across the world, ranking No.&#160;3 in the US, No.&#160;2 in Canada, No.&#160;5 in the United Kingdom, and No.&#160;3 in Portugal, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> The album has sold over 285,000 copies in the US and 600,000 copies worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> High Violet is now a <a href="/wiki/Gold_Album" title="Gold Album" class="mw-redirect">Gold Album</a> in Canada, Ireland, Denmark, Belgium, Australia and the United Kingdom. On October 25, 2010, it was awarded a Q award, presented by <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Sumner" title="Bernard Sumner">Bernard Sumner</a> of <a href="/wiki/Joy_Division" title="Joy Division">Joy Division</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Order" title="New Order">New Order</a>, for Best Album.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> During 2011, the band capitalized on the success of <i>High Violet</i> and toured extensively in North America, Europe and Australia, including festivals in Ireland, Germany and Bohemia.</p>
<p>On May 13, 2010, the band appeared on the <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i>, performing "Afraid of Everyone".<sup id="cite_ref-http:.2F.2Fwww.cbs.com.2Flate_night.2Flate_show.2Fvideo.2F.3Fpid.3D_kL2O5tXB4_1Y9Jki5grV6DztCqaO0aY.26vs.3DDefault.26play.3Dtrue_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http:.2F.2Fwww.cbs.com.2Flate_night.2Flate_show.2Fvideo.2F.3Fpid.3D_kL2O5tXB4_1Y9Jki5grV6DztCqaO0aY.26vs.3DDefault.26play.3Dtrue-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> On July 7, 2010, it debuted a new, unreleased track titled "You Were a Kindness" at the Tivoli in the Netherlands.</p>
<p>In 2011, The National were nominated for a Brit Award for International Breakthrough Act and an <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> Performing Woodie.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> Two of its songs, "Start a War" (from <i><a href="/wiki/Boxer_(album)" title="Boxer (album)">Boxer</a></i>) and "About Today", were featured on the critically acclaimed film <i><a href="/wiki/Warrior_(2011_film)" title="Warrior (2011 film)">Warrior</a></i>.</p>
<p>On March 9, 2011 The National released a music video for Conversation 16 featuring <a href="/wiki/John_Slattery" title="John Slattery">John Slattery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kristen_Schaal" title="Kristen Schaal">Kristen Schaal</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Urbaniak" title="James Urbaniak">James Urbaniak</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> Two days later <a href="/wiki/Valve_Corporation" title="Valve Corporation">Valve Corporation</a> announced the band would be contributing an original song, titled "Exile Vilify", for the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Portal_2" title="Portal 2">Portal 2</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> and on April 12, 2011, the band released "Think You Can Wait" from the soundtrack of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Win_Win_(film)" title="Win Win (film)">Win Win</a></i>.</p>
<p>On April 20, 2011, The National played Starlight Theater in Kansas City, Missouri. During the show, the band played "About Today", and dedicated the song in memory of Gerard Smith of TV on the Radio, who had died earlier that day of lung cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On December 8, 2011 the band performed on Q (the CBC radio show) in front of a live audience. Two new songs were performed for the first time: "Rylan" and "I Need My Girl". The third song performed was "Vanderlyle Crybaby Geeks" which featured Justin Vernon of <a href="/wiki/Bon_Iver" title="Bon Iver">Bon Iver</a> who was also on the show.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On March 24, 2012, members of the band, Scott Devendorf, Bryan Devendorf and <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Dessner" title="Aaron Dessner">Aaron Dessner</a>, performed with <a href="/wiki/Bob_Weir" title="Bob Weir">Bob Weir</a> in a live broadcast concert, <i>The Bridge Session</i>, at Weir's <a href="/wiki/TRI_Studios" title="TRI Studios" class="mw-redirect">TRI Studios</a> to bring awareness for <a href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" title="Non-profit organization" class="mw-redirect">non-profit organization</a>, <a href="/wiki/HeadCount" title="HeadCount">HeadCount</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The National recorded <i><a href="/wiki/Music_of_Game_of_Thrones#The_Rains_of_Castamere" title="Music of Game of Thrones">The Rains of Castamere</a></i> for the <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i> season 2 episode "<a href="/wiki/Blackwater_(Game_of_Thrones)" title="Blackwater (Game of Thrones)">Blackwater</a>".</p>
<p>They also recorded a cover of the song <i><a href="/wiki/I%27ll_See_You_in_My_Dreams_(song)" title="I'll See You in My Dreams (song)">I'll See You in My Dreams</a></i> for the HBO TV series <a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Empire" title="Boardwalk Empire">Boardwalk Empire</a>, and a cover of the "Thanksgiving Song" from the Fox TV series <a href="/wiki/Bob%27s_Burgers" title="Bob's Burgers">Bob's Burgers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On December 9, 2012, they debuted three new songs "I Should Live in Salt", "Humiliation", and "Graceless" at the All Tomorrow's Parties event in Camber Sands, United Kingdom.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trouble_Will_Find_Me_and_Mistaken_for_Strangers_.282013.E2.80.93present.29"><i>Trouble Will Find Me</i> and <i>Mistaken for Strangers</i> (2013–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Trouble Will Find Me and Mistaken for Strangers (2013–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_Will_Find_Me" title="Trouble Will Find Me">Trouble Will Find Me</a></i> was released via 4AD Records on May 21, 2013 in the US to widespread critical acclaim. The album debuted with first week sales topping charts across the world, ranking No. 3 in the US, Canada and the UK, among others. In its first week the album sold 74,722 copies in the US. Following the release, the band has embarked on a world tour in support of the album. <i><a href="/wiki/Trouble_Will_Find_Me" title="Trouble Will Find Me">Trouble Will Find Me</a></i> earned a Best Alternative Album nomination in the 2014 Grammys.</p>
<p>The album title was announced on February 25, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> The title, tracklist and album art were revealed on March 21. The band released the video for 'Demons', the first single from the album, on YouTube on April 8.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> During a Reddit AMA on May 8 they premiered the video for "Sea of Love." <sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On February 28, 2013, they announced the upcoming release of a documentary chronicling the lead singer's brother Tom Berninger's journey of touring with the band. The film, titled <i>Mistaken for Strangers,</i> premiered at New York City's <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a> on April 17.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> "Mistaken for Strangers" has been described as "a funny, eccentric and finally deeply poignant depiction of art, family, ­self-sabotage and the prickly intricacies of brotherly love."<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013 they contributed the song "Lean" to the <a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Catching_Fire_%E2%80%93_Original_Motion_Picture_Soundtrack" title="The Hunger Games: Catching Fire – Original Motion Picture Soundtrack">official soundtrack</a> for the movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hunger_Games:_Catching_Fire" title="The Hunger Games: Catching Fire">The Hunger Games: Catching Fire</a></i>.</p>
<p>The band performed "Graceless" and "I Need My Girl" on the March 8, 2014 episode of <i>Saturday Night Live</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>"Fireproof" was used in 2 episodes of the USA network's "Covert Affairs" Season 4 - "Vamos" and "River Euphrates".</p>
<p>"Hard to Find" was used in an episode of the USA network's "Suits" Season 3 - "Conflict of Interest"</p>
<p>In a 2014 interview with Postmedia News, Berninger said it's unlikely The National will release a new album until 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>"England" was used in an Apple iPhone 5S commercial during the 2014 World Cup.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_process">Writing process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Writing process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aaron and Bryce Dessner write and develop the music which Matt Berninger then responds to, building lyrics off of the music Aaron and Bryce have written.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> Matt has said that the melody and the rhythm come first and then words and imagery will start to stick to a melody. “I never sit and fill a journal with lyrics.”<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Band_members">Band members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Band members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>Matt Berninger - lead vocals (1999-present)</li>
<li>Aaron Dessner - guitar, bass, piano, keyboards, harmonica, backing vocals (1999-present)</li>
<li>Bryce Dessner - guitar, keyboards, backing vocals (2001-present)</li>
<li>Bryan Devendorf - drums, percussion, backing vocals (1999-present)</li>
<li>Scott Devendorf - bass, guitar, backing vocals (1999-present)</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In support of <a href="/wiki/High_Violet" title="High Violet">High Violet</a>, The National spent summer 2011 playing a wide range of festivals including Heineken <a href="/wiki/Open%27er_Festival" title="Open'er Festival">Open'er Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reading_and_Leeds_Festivals" title="Reading and Leeds Festivals">Reading and Leeds Festivals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sziget_Festival" title="Sziget Festival">Sziget Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_Werchter" title="Rock Werchter">Rock Werchter</a> Festival, <a href="/wiki/Skanderborg_Festival" title="Skanderborg Festival">Skanderborg Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_Picnic" title="Electric Picnic">Electric Picnic</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.openairsg.ch/">St. Gallen Open Air Festival</a>. Summer 2011 also saw The National take a turn headlining their first major European festival, <a href="/wiki/Latitude_Festival" title="Latitude Festival">Latitude</a>.</p>
<p>In October 2011, The National discussed their career with <a href="/wiki/Atul_Gawande" title="Atul Gawande">Dr. Atul Gawande</a> and performed an acoustic set with string quartet at the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.newyorker.com/festival/">New Yorker Festival</a>. Other participants in the festival included luminaries like <a href="/wiki/Jhumpa_Lahiri" title="Jhumpa Lahiri">Jhumpa Lahiri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colson_Whitehead" title="Colson Whitehead">Colson Whitehead</a>, and the entire cast of <a href="/wiki/Arrested_Development_(TV_series)" title="Arrested Development (TV series)">Arrested Development</a> among others.</p>
<p>In December 2012, The National curated All Tomorrow's Parties festival in Camber Sands.</p>
<p>The National played at a few major festivals in the summer of 2013, including The Boston Calling Music Festival in May, <a href="/wiki/Bonnaroo" title="Bonnaroo" class="mw-redirect">Bonnaroo</a> in June, <a href="/wiki/Bunbury_Music_Festival" title="Bunbury Music Festival">Bunbury Music Festival</a> in Cincinnati and two of the biggest European festivals <a href="/wiki/Roskilde_Festival" title="Roskilde Festival">Roskilde Festival</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rock_Werchter" title="Rock Werchter">Rock Werchter</a> in July. They then returned to North America for <a href="/wiki/Lollapalooza" title="Lollapalooza">Lollapalooza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Outside_Lands_Music_and_Arts_Festival" title="Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival">Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival</a> in August,<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/St_Jerome%27s_Laneway_Festival" title="St Jerome's Laneway Festival">St. Jerome's Laneway Festival</a> in September,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> and Austin City Limits in October.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> It was announced that The National will return to headline The Boston Calling Music Festival in September 2014, along with <a href="/wiki/Lorde" title="Lorde">Lorde</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Replacements_(band)" title="The Replacements (band)">The Replacements</a><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The National played 3 consecutive shows at the Celebrate Brooklyn Music Festival in June 2014.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_and_social_support">Political and social support</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Political and social support">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_Safe_Space_NYC">2008 Safe Space NYC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2008 Safe Space NYC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008 the band designed a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.yellowbirdproject.com/products/the-national">t-shirt</a><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> for the <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Bird_Project" title="Yellow Bird Project">Yellow Bird Project</a> to raise money for Safe Space NYC, an organization which provides safe refuge for underprivileged children and families in Southeast Queens.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2008_US_presidential_election">2008 US presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2008 US presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The band supported <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2008_United_States_presidential_election" title="2008 United States presidential election" class="mw-redirect">presidential candidacy</a> in 2008. In July of that year, the band designed and sold a t-shirt featuring Obama's image above the words "Mr. November," a reference to both their song from <i><a href="/wiki/Alligator_(The_National_album)" title="Alligator (The National album)">Alligator</a></i> and the month of the U.S. presidential election. All proceeds were donated to Obama's campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On October 16, 2008, The National played a rally for Barack Obama on <a href="/wiki/Fountain_Square,_Cincinnati" title="Fountain Square, Cincinnati">Fountain Square</a> in their hometown of Cincinnati with <a href="/wiki/The_Breeders" title="The Breeders">The Breeders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-citybeat1_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-citybeat1-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> Buses for early voting were available before the show to take voters to the Hamilton County Board of Elections.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_mid_term_election">2010 mid term election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2010 mid term election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 28, 2010, The National played in front of over 25,000 people before a speech by President Obama at a rally in <a href="/wiki/Madison,_Wisconsin" title="Madison, Wisconsin">Madison, Wisconsin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_US_presidential_election">2012 US presidential election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2012 US presidential election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The National were also involved with efforts to reelect President Barack Obama in the 2012 election. On September 1, 2012 The National opened for the President at a campaign rally in Urbandale, Iowa in front of a crowd in excess of 10,000 people.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> In October the band traveled to Ohio where they played two free shows and visited university campuses as part of Barack Obama’s GottaVote campaign to encourage young adults to register to vote and to cast their vote for Obama.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Was_the_Night"><i>Dark Was the Night</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Dark Was the Night">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On February 17, 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Was_the_Night" title="Dark Was the Night">Dark Was the Night</a></i>, the twentieth <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a> compilation, was released by <a href="/wiki/4AD" title="4AD">4AD</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-drownedinsound1_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drownedinsound1-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gigwise_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gigwise-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TheSkinny_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheSkinny-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> Curated by Aaron and Bryce Dessner, this album comprised songs by bands and artists such as <a href="/wiki/Arcade_Fire" title="Arcade Fire">Arcade Fire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grizzly_Bear_(band)" title="Grizzly Bear (band)">Grizzly Bear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beirut_(band)" title="Beirut (band)">Beirut</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Byrne" title="David Byrne">David Byrne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sufjan_Stevens" title="Sufjan Stevens">Sufjan Stevens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spoon_(band)" title="Spoon (band)">Spoon</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Dirty_Projectors" title="The Dirty Projectors" class="mw-redirect">The Dirty Projectors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Feist_(singer)" title="Feist (singer)">Feist</a> and The National, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-drownedinsound1_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-drownedinsound1-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gigwise_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gigwise-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TheSkinny_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheSkinny-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup> Over one million dollars of the proceeds from album sales were donated to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Organization" title="Red Hot Organization">Red Hot Organization</a>, an international organization dedicated to fighting <a href="/wiki/AIDS" title="AIDS" class="mw-redirect">AIDS</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On May 3, 2009, Aaron and Bryce Dessner curated a concert for <i>Dark Was the Night</i> at <a href="/wiki/Radio_City_Music_Hall" title="Radio City Music Hall">Radio City Music Hall</a> in New York City. Performers included <a href="/wiki/David_Byrne" title="David Byrne">David Byrne</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Dirty_Projectors" title="The Dirty Projectors" class="mw-redirect">The Dirty Projectors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Feist_(singer)" title="Feist (singer)">Feist</a>, The National, and several other artists who contributed to the compilation.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> Red Hot Organization founder and director, <a href="/wiki/John_Carlin_(businessman)" title="John Carlin (businessman)">John Carlin</a>, was quoted as saying, "<i>Dark Was the Night</i> encapsulated the spirit and creativity of a new generation of musicians whose work struck a chord and got people to actually purchase the album and raise hundreds of thousands of dollars to fight AIDS."<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_benefit_concerts">Other benefit concerts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_National_(band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Other benefit concerts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
